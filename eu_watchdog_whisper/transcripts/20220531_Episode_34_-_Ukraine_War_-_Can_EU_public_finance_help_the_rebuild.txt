The EBRD was established in 1991 with a mandate to promote democracy and market economics in the formerly centrally planned economies of Central Eastern Europe and the former Soviet Union. The general idea was that it would help privatise the industry in these countries, build new infrastructure connecting it to the rest of the world, to Western Europe, and help integrate these countries into the international community.
